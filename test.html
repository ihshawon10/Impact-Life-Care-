Who We Are

More Than a Service Provider

Impact Life Care is more than a service provider; we are your dedicated partners in navigating the complexities of the NDIS. Located in the heart of Melbourne, we specialise in delivering high-quality, reliable support that respects your choices and honours your individuality.

Our team is driven by the fundamental belief that everyone deserves to live a fulfilling, inclusive life regardless of the challenges they may face. We understand that every journey is different, which is why we take a collaborative approach to care, ensuring that our participants and their families are at the centre of every decision we make.

By bridging the gap between clinical support and community connection, we create a nurturing environment where goals are not just set, but actively achieved. Our local expertise allows us to tap into Melbourne's rich resources, providing you with opportunities for growth that are both relevant and accessible.

Whether you are seeking to increase your independence at home, develop new skills for the future, or engage more deeply with your local community, Impact Life Care stands as a pillar of support. We are committed to excellence, transparency, and the continuous improvement of our services.

Six Programs, Infinite Possibilities

Each program is built to open new doors — whether that's a job, a community, a healthy body, or a safe home.

🎓

Education

Learning & Growth

Education refers to access to learning opportunities at all levels — school, TAFE, university, vocational training, or informal life skills education. Under the NDIS, this may also involve support to access learning environments or achieve educational goals.

Why It's Important:

Education opens doors to personal growth, independence, and improved life choices. For clients with disability, it fosters inclusion, confidence, and prepares them for active participation in society.

How NDIS Clients Benefit:

  • Tailored learning supports (e.g., educational aides, assistive technology)
  • Increased confidence and sense of achievement
  • Pathways to further education, employment, or community participation
  • Development of critical life and communication skills

Education providers suitable for clients with disabilities include inclusion schools. Hester Hornbrook Academy by MCM provides such services.

Employment

Work & Purpose

This includes access to employment support services, job coaching, workplace modifications, and skill-building programs. It also encompasses supported employment opportunities through NDIS funding.

Why It's Important:

Employment offers a sense of purpose, routine, and independence. For people with disability, suitable work opportunities break down barriers and enable social and economic inclusion.

How NDIS Clients Benefit:

  • Enhanced independence and financial freedom
  • Opportunities to contribute meaningfully to society
  • Development of job skills and workplace confidence
  • Support with transitioning from school to work or re-entering the workforce

Organisations that assist clients find work include Wise Employment, Mission Australia and Ability Works Australia.

Health and Wellbeing

Health & Wellness

This includes both physical and mental health, access to therapy (e.g., occupational therapy, speech therapy, psychology), health services, and overall wellness strategies.

Why It's Important:

Wellbeing is foundational for living a good life. Many NDIS participants face complex health needs, and a coordinated approach ensures they can live more comfortably and independently.

How NDIS Clients Benefit:

  • Better management of disability-related health conditions
  • Access to allied health supports tailored to their goals
  • Improved emotional resilience and reduced risk of isolation or burnout
  • Supports that promote independence in daily self-care and mobility

We work with nutritionists, doctors and allied health providers to ensure our clients' health and wellbeing is well taken care of.

Civic Participation

Community & Advocacy

Civic participation involves taking part in community life, such as voting, volunteering, advocacy, joining clubs or groups, and having a say in decisions that affect one's life.

Why It's Important:

Being involved in civic life fosters inclusion, empowerment, and representation. It helps NDIS participants build a sense of identity, contribution, and agency in society.

How NDIS Clients Benefit:

  • Increased self-confidence and visibility in the community
  • Opportunities to make decisions and express opinions
  • Greater social justice and advocacy awareness
  • Development of leadership skills and a stronger sense of purpose

We ensure all clients are allowed to vote, advocate for their rights through the NDIS Commission and join local community clubs like Salvation Army for volunteer work.

🤗

Social Connections

Belonging & Inclusion

This refers to building and maintaining relationships with family, friends, carers, and community networks. NDIS supports might include social groups, mentoring, or support workers to facilitate inclusion.

Why It's Important:

Social connection is a key determinant of mental health and happiness. Many people with disability are at higher risk of social isolation, which impacts their emotional wellbeing.

How NDIS Clients Benefit:

  • Enhanced sense of belonging and self-worth
  • Development of communication and interpersonal skills
  • Opportunities to form meaningful relationships
  • Reduced loneliness and increased participation in daily life

We allow clients to go on one-to-one outings with support workers, attend day placement services and encourage them to visit their families where possible.

Housing and Living

Home & Independence

This involves access to safe, accessible, and suitable accommodation, including Supported Independent Living (SIL), Specialist Disability Accommodation (SDA), or assistance with daily living tasks in one's own home.

Why It's Important:

A stable and appropriate living environment is crucial for health, safety, and independence. It also enables greater control over one's life and personal space.

How NDIS Clients Benefit:

  • Improved stability and safety in daily life
  • Increased autonomy through supports for daily living
  • Opportunities to live independently or with chosen companions
  • Customised housing solutions that cater to individual needs and abilities

We work with companies like Nextt, MyCareSpace and HomeCaring to ensure clients find the right housing solution.
